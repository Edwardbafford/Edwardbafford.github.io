Kb, kB, kiB, and KB

Some quick definitions for digital information metrics.

Bit

Let's start off simple, a bit is the most basic unit of digital information, which is represented by a single binary value, a one or a zero.

Byte

Okay, next a byte is a series of 8 bits. Simple enough!

Kilobit (kb)

A kilobit is 1000 bits.

Kilobyte (kB)

A kilobyte is 1000 bytes making it 8 times larger than a kilobit.

Kilobytes and Kilobits follow the standard metric patterns where kilo is 1000, mega is 1000000, giga is 1000000000 and so on...

Kibibyte

Now, Kibibyte is the weird one.. it represents 1024 bytes. It's close to 1000 and therefore close to a kilo but it can be represented by 2 to the power of 10. The 2 to the 10th thing I suppose lines up nicely with digital hardware or something? I don't know but Kibibytes are more commonly used than Kilobytes in computers and are abbreviated as kiB or KB.

Following the metric pattern a mebibyte is 1024 x 1024 bytes, a gebibyte is 1024 x 1024 x 1024 bytes, etc. The abbreviation patterns are kept as well miB/MB, giB/GB, etc.
