General Data Protection Regulation

The GDPR is a regulatory bill that was created by the European Union. The bill defines individual's rights with respect to their personal data.

Rights

Consent - data can only be processed with explicit consent from the user

Purpose - how the data will be used must be specifically and explicitly defined and adhered to

Accuracy - the integrity of the data must be maintained

Security - the users data must be protected

Responsibility - the company must be able to prove compliance to the previous principles

Scope

The GDPR technically applies to any personal data originating from the EU

Personal Data - information that can directly or indirectly identify a person or characteristics about them

  • Names
  • IDs
  • Geographical information

Non-personal data examples

  • Anonymous data
  • Company information or IDs
  • Personal information of someone deceased
